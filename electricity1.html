Key Benefits

  • Reduce Carbon Footprint: Solar power as an alternative to fossil fuels to lower air pollution.
  • Sustainable Waste Management: Efficient management of electronic waste from solar panels.
  • Energy Access: Provide renewable energy to off-grid areas for sustainable development.

Algorithms Used

  • Dijkstra's Algorithm: Find the shortest path for efficient energy distribution networks. This algorithm is ideal for minimizing the distance or time taken for energy transmission, ensuring that electricity reaches areas with minimal losses.
  • Greedy Algorithm: Optimize placement of solar panels for maximum efficiency. The greedy approach ensures that each panel is placed in the best available location at each step, considering factors like sunlight exposure and shading.
  • Prim's Algorithm: Design cost-effective power grids using minimum spanning tree methods. This algorithm helps in creating the least-cost connections between multiple solar power sources and consumers, ensuring energy distribution is both efficient and affordable.
  • Sorting Algorithms (e.g., QuickSort): Prioritize tasks such as resource allocation and panel installation. Sorting algorithms like QuickSort help in managing tasks efficiently by sorting them based on priority, which is crucial in large-scale solar power projects.
  • Dynamic Programming: Solve complex problems like scheduling and energy storage optimization. This approach is used to handle problems where decisions need to be made sequentially, like deciding when to store energy and when to distribute it across the grid.
