City Designing: Police Station

Creating accessible, safe, and inclusive public spaces for all

Case Study: Police Station

This case study focuses on ensuring that police stations serve as safe, inclusive, and accessible spaces, especially for vulnerable groups such as women, children, older persons, and persons with disabilities. The aim is to design police stations that promote safety and security for all citizens.

Target 11.7: By 2030, provide universal access to safe, inclusive, and accessible, green, and public spaces, particularly for women and children, older persons, and persons with disabilities.

Indicator 11.7.2: Proportion of persons victimized by physical or sexual harassment, by sex, age, disability status, and place of occurrence.

Key Benefits

  • Public Safety: Ensuring police stations are designed with accessible spaces to promote a sense of safety and security for everyone.
  • Inclusivity: Designing police stations to be fully accessible to all individuals, including those with physical disabilities.
  • Protection of Vulnerable Groups: Providing specific provisions for women, children, and other vulnerable groups to ensure their protection and comfort in police stations.

Algorithms Used

  • DFS (Depth First Search): A graph traversal algorithm used to explore nodes and edges of a graph.
  • BFS (Breadth First Search): A graph traversal algorithm that explores nodes level by level.
  • Bubble Sort: A simple sorting algorithm that compares adjacent elements and swaps them if necessary.
  • Quick Sort: A divide-and-conquer sorting algorithm that selects a pivot and partitions the array around it.
  • Dijkstra's Algorithm: An algorithm for finding the shortest path between nodes in a graph.
