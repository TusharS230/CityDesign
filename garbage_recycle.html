Garbage Recycling and Waste Management

In urban environments, managing waste and ensuring effective recycling practices is key to reducing environmental impact. Recycling helps minimize landfill waste and promotes a circular economy by reusing materials.

Target 11.6: By 2030, reduce the adverse per capita environmental impact of cities, including by paying special attention to air quality and municipal and other waste management.

Indicator 11.6.1: Proportion of urban solid waste regularly collected and with adequate final discharge out of total urban solid waste generated, by cities.

Key Benefits

  • Reduce Landfill Waste: Efficient waste management to reduce the amount of waste sent to landfills.
  • Improve Recycling Rates: Promoting recycling to reuse materials and decrease pollution.
  • Energy Recovery: Using waste-to-energy technologies to generate power from garbage.

Algorithms Used

  • Greedy Algorithm: Optimize waste collection routes to minimize transportation costs.
  • Dynamic Programming: Plan recycling schedules for maximum efficiency.
  • Machine Learning: Predict waste generation patterns and automate waste sorting.
  • Graph Algorithms: Model waste flow in the city and optimize recycling networks.
